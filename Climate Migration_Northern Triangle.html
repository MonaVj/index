CLIMATE MIGRATION IN THE NORTHERN TRIANGLE

How is climate change affecting migration in Central America Northern Triangle?

Forced migration due to climate change will present one of the most severe challenges to the resilience of communities forced to migrate as well as to local and national governments. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) predicts that 150 million people will be displaced by 2050.

Erosion, flooding, and sea level rise will be the primary causes of displacement. Water and food security issues, due to drought and salt water intrusion, will also impact the sustainability of communities. Understanding how climate change is affecting communities could allow to proactive planning, grounded in equity and justice, to reduce harm and save lives.

Moreover, this could help governments to create legal pathways for migration in order to protect communities to respond quicker and better to climate-induced human migration.
